eBay sells StubHub to Viagogo for $4.05 billion

After a lengthy consultation period sparked by hedge fund and activist investor Elliott Management, eBay has confirmed that it’s offloading its ticket marketplace StubHub to Viagogo in an all-cash deal worth $4.08 billion.

This represents a substantial markup on the $310 million it paid for the company back in 2007, while it represents a 400% premium on the roughly $1.2 billion StubHub generates in revenue annually.

“We believe this transaction is a great outcome and maximizes long-term value for eBay shareholders,” noted eBay’s interim CEO Scott Schenkel in a press release.

Transitions San Jose-based eBay has been mulling a major transition for the best part of a year, after Elliott Management — which owns around 4% of eBay — suggested that the company would be able to generate far more value if it was to sell or spin out its various components into separate businesses. The idea behind this was that by focusing on its marketplace business, which currently generates most of eBay’s revenues, it would be able to improve its core business without distractions.

The other two components constituting the main eBay business were its classifieds unit, which operates under various brands globally such as Gumtree in the U.K, and StubHub, which we now know is being sold to Viagogo in a deal that should close in Q1 2020.

Certainly, eBay has recent form in spinning out companies. Back in 2014 it revealed plans to spin out its payments business, PayPal, into a standalone company following a long and contentious feud with another activist investor called Carl Icahn, and in 2015 eBay finally made PayPal a standalone company once again. For context on the spinout’s success, PayPal now has a market cap of $120 billion, roughly 4 times that of its former parent company.

The ongoing divestment saga led CEO Devin Wenig to step down back in September, with CFO Schenkel stepping in on a temporary basis. Wenig tweeted at the time that his reason for leaving was that he wasn’t seeing eye-to-eye with the board on the company’s new direction, so it seemed fairly clear that something was set to be announced in the following months.

“Over the past several months, eBay’s leadership team and board of directors have been engaged in a thorough review of our current strategies and portfolio, and we concluded that this was the best path forward for both eBay and StubHub,” Schenkel said. “We firmly believe in the StubHub business and we are excited about its future growth potential with Viagogo as its owner.”

While the synergies between StubHub and Viagogo are obvious, given that they both operate event ticket marketplaces, it’s worth noting here that StubHub and Viagogo also share a cofounder. Indeed, Eric Baker helped create StubHub while at Stanford University back in 2000, but he departed before it was bought by eBay in 2007. Baker later Viagogo out of Europe in 2006, and has been CEO ever since.

“It has long been my wish to unite the two companies,” Baker said. “I am so proud of how StubHub has grown over the years and excited about the possibilities for our shared future. Buyers will have a wider choice of tickets, and sellers will have a wider network of buyers. Bringing these two companies together creates a win-win for fans — more choice and better pricing.”
